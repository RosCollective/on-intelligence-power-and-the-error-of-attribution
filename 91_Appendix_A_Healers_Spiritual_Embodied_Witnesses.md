APPENDIX A
Healers, Spiritual and Embodied Witnesses

Chronological list by earliest date with geography
Format: Name (dates) | Role | Geography | Contextual harm

---

Pythia of Delphi (-800–-500)
Role: Oracle, spiritual authority
Geography: Ancient Greece
Contextual harm: Authority systematically displaced and extinguished through institutional religious consolidation, eliminating autonomous female spiritual authority

European Midwives & Herbalists (1450–1750)
Role: Midwives, herbal healers
Geography: Europe
Contextual harm: Executed or persecuted during witch hunts targeting embodied female knowledge and reproductive authority

Indigenous Healers (Americas) (1500–1900)
Role: Healers, shamans
Geography: Americas
Contextual harm: Suppressed, criminalized, and killed under colonial religious and medical regimes, with knowledge systems deliberately eradicated

Teresa of Ávila (1515–1582)
Role: Mystic, reformer
Geography: Spain
Contextual harm: Investigated, surveilled, and constrained by the Inquisition, with spiritual authority constrained under strict institutional control

Giordano Bruno (1548–1600)
Role: Mystic philosopher
Geography: Italy
Contextual harm: Executed for cosmological and theological heresy

African Traditional Healers (1600–1900)
Role: Healers, spiritual leaders
Geography: Africa
Contextual harm: Criminalized, imprisoned, and executed under colonial rule, with healing practices recast as criminal superstition

Efua Dorkenoo (1949–2014)
Role: FGM abolitionist, community health advocate
Geography: Ghana / United Kingdom
Contextual harm: Lifelong threats, exile, and institutional resistance while dismantling entrenched ritual violence, with sustained personal risk until death

Tibetan Buddhist Monastics (1950–)
Role: Religious practitioners
Geography: Tibet / China
Contextual harm: Imprisonment, execution, exile, and enforced disappearance following suppression of unapproved religion

Waris Dirie (1965–)
Role: Human rights advocate, former UN ambassador
Geography: Somalia / Global
Contextual harm: Exile, sustained threats, and ongoing personal risk for public opposition to ritualized violence, despite international recognition

Anti‑FGM Community Defenders (collective) (1970–)
Role: Community educators, healers, advocates
Geography: East & West Africa / Global Diaspora
Contextual harm: Threats, imprisonment, exile, and targeted killings for opposing ritualized gendered violence

Berta Cáceres (1971–2016)
Role: Indigenous environmental and spiritual leader
Geography: Honduras
Contextual harm: Assassinated for defending land and water as sacred

Amazon Indigenous Land Defenders (1980–)
Role: Indigenous environmental defenders
Geography: Brazil / Amazon Basin
Contextual harm: Ongoing targeted killings, displacement, and criminalization for opposing extractive industries and defending land as sacred

Standing Rock Water Protectors (2016–)
Role: Indigenous land and water defenders
Geography: United States (Dakota Access Pipeline)
Contextual harm: Militarized police violence, mass arrests, surveillance, and ongoing legal persecution for asserting Indigenous spiritual and land-based authority
